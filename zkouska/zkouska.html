Mathematical formulas

Linear Equation

A linear equation is an equation that may be put in the form

ax2+ b = 0

where a and b are real numbers and x is a variable. This form is sonetimes called the stsndart form of a linear equation. Note that most linear equations will not start off in this form. Also, the variable may or not be an x so don't get too locked into always seeing an x there.

Quadratic Equation

The standard form of a quadratic equation looks like this:

ax2 + bx + c = 0

where a,b,c is the variable or unknown (we don't know it yet). Also, a can't be 0.

The area of a circle (A)

π (Pi) times the radius (r) squared:

A = π × r2

or, when you know the diameter (D):

A = (π / 4) × D2

or, when you know the circumference (C):

A = C2 / 4 × π

Intersection

The intersection of two sets A and B, denoted by A ∩ B, is the set of all objects htat are members of both the sets A and B. In symbols,

A ∩ B = { x : x ∈ A and x ∈ B }

That is, x an element of the intersection A ∩ B if and only x is both an element of A and an element of B
